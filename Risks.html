The Risks of Using Social Media
and Their Countermeasures

Risks Social Media Users Might Face

Phishing attacks/scams

This online scam preys on people by sending them an email that appears to be from a reputable organization. It requests personal identification data from the customer. Then, they take advantage of the data by accessing the customer's active accounts.

Unethical Data Gathering

It occurs when Companies store and exploit all their customer's private information to target better advertisement to them. Also, companies occasionally pass on consumers' personal data to unaffiliated parties without their knowledge or permission.

Cyberbullying

It is a form of bullying that occurs online. It happens by sharing hurtful and malicious content about someone else. It can also involve disclosing sensitive or private information about another individual in a way that causes embarrassment or humiliation.

Automated Bot Attacks

It occurs when "social media bot" accounts that constantly post and follow new users or "botnets" spread spam messages, steal data, and execute attacks of distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) that give hackers opportunity to access people's networks and gadgets.

How to Countermeasure/Mitigate the Risks
  • Make sure that only reliable sources can access private information by adjusting your privacy settings all the way up.

  • Be aware and up to date to the company's privacy policies on their website and all of their social media platforms.

  • When using or installing any applications, make sure to check the request permissions carefully.

  • When using social media, never share and disclose any of your private information than what is absolutely necessary i.e. your name, date of birth, your home address, your personal photos, where you live, and etc.

  • Never respond to emails or click links that you think are suspicious, and double check if the sender is legit or a scam by looking for grammar or spelling mistakes and if the domain name is correct or not.

  • Install an antivirus software/firewalls, to prevent malicious viruses and third-party users/ hackers to enter your computer.
