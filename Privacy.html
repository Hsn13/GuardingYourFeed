Privacy

Privacy Policy



Our entire online activity is monitored and saved in a database somewhere; every click, search, or double tap is documented and eventually related back to you. A lot of people are not aware of the technological complexity of information privacy. Which is why a platform is required by law to publish their Privacy Policies on their websites and inform the client how his/her data is being collected, handled, and processed.


Importance of Privacy Policies

In terms of business, In terms of client,

Companies absolutely must include a privacy policy on the website, and/or platform as it is required by law. This not only shows what data is collected and how it is processed, but it induces a feeling of trust and assurance for your clients knowing how their data is being managed. Plus, when your customer's data is protected, your organization is also protected. In other words, you protect your reputation and yourself from disastrous lawsuits by protecting customer privacy.

Companies use lawyers to ensure that their privacy policy is in accordance with all applicable state and federal regulations. However, the excessive use of jargon language confuses the reader and leaves no choice but to ignore and skip the reading. The habit of agreeing to Terms & Conditions without actually reading might give a business the right to access data and handle them as pleased. It is important to read and understand the content of a privacy policy before agreeing on any terms or starting an account.


Best Practice


Despite all laws and regulations that protect the physical or digital right to privacy, its common that issues arise due to user’s activity and behavior. For instance, a user must not share critical private information like location or credit card information, as the platform is not responsible for any negative outcomes that might be caused for sharing such information. Social Media users must raise their awareness to what information they should and shouldn’t share online as it can potentially be used to access/damage your account and/or information. Hence, here is a list of practices to follow when using social media

  • Read and understand privacy policies before accepting
  • Don’t Post anything you will regret later on. And remember: ONCE POSTED, ALWAYS POSTED
  • Maintain a positive harmless online presence
  • Don’t post sensitive information
  • Protect your device with Antivirus and regular updates
  • Block and Report accounts/threats that you run into
  • Know when to take further action with the law or police
  • Use strong Preventive, Detective, and Corrective controls to protect your account
  • Raise your awareness on phishing, hacking, blackmail, and other exploitation methods
